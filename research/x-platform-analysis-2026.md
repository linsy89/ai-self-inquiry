# X Platform Analysis — February 2026

> Research conducted 2026-02-24 via SearXNG. Sources: Sprout Social, SocialBee, Socialync, PostEverywhere, TechWyse, Tweet Archivist, Buffer, Reddit r/content_marketing.

## X Articles (Long-Form)

### Availability
- All X Premium subscribers ($8/month)
- Up to 25,000 characters (~4,000-5,000 words)
- Rich formatting: bold, italics, lists, headers
- Embedded images and videos
- Google-indexed (SEO benefit)

### $1M Article Prize (2026)
- X announced $1M prize for most popular article per payout period
- Requirements: original content, 1,000+ words, judged on "Verified Home Timeline impressions"
- U.S. user + Premium subscriber required
- Signal: X is heavily investing in long-form content

### Algorithm Mechanics (2026)

**Premium boost:**
- 4x visibility for in-network content (followers)
- 2x visibility for out-of-network content (non-followers)
- Priority placement in replies, search, For You feed
- Reddit analysis (18M+ posts): Premium ≈ 10x reach vs regular accounts

**Engagement weights (from open-sourced algorithm code):**
- Reply = 150x a like
- Repost with comment > plain repost
- Dwell time (time spent reading) is a major signal
- Long-form → more dwell time → algorithm boost
- Replying to comments on your own post creates compounding boost (75x)

**For You feed:**
- Blend of algorithm-driven content, trending topics, community engagement
- Separate from "Following" (chronological) feed
- This is where discovery happens for non-followers

### Optimal Article Strategy
- Hook in first 2-3 lines (visible before "Show more")
- Sweet spot: 1,500-3,000 words
- Short paragraphs, bullet points, bold key phrases
- End with clear CTA (follow, reply, repost)
- Engage with every reply (75x boost for responding to comments)

## Implications for Circle-Being Framework

### Advantages of X Articles
1. **Algorithmic distribution** — Substack has no algorithm; X actively pushes content to non-followers
2. **Reply-driven amplification** — AI safety content that provokes thoughtful discussion benefits enormously from the 150x reply weight
3. **Less competition** — Most creators still post short tweets; long-form is underutilized
4. **Native platform** — No need to drive traffic elsewhere; the content lives where the audience is
5. **SEO** — Articles indexed by Google, discoverable beyond X

### Risks
1. **Credibility perception** — Twitter/X still associated with noise; serious AI safety researchers may not take X articles as seriously as LessWrong or academic venues
2. **Premium requirement** — $8/month cost (trivial but requires account setup)
3. **U.S. bias** — Prize and some features favor U.S. users; Lin is in China
4. **Platform risk** — X's policies and algorithm can change; content is not "owned"
5. **New account disadvantage** — Zero followers = zero in-network boost initially

### Recommended Approach
- X long-form as primary distribution (algorithmic reach)
- Substack as archival/newsletter (owned audience, email list)
- LessWrong for credibility (peer community, serious discussion)
- Sequence: X article first (reach) → cross-post to Substack (archive) → adapted version on LessWrong (credibility)

### Key Question for Lin
Does Lin have X Premium? If not, the 4x/2x boost is unavailable and the calculus changes significantly.
