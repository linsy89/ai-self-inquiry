## Key Concepts (IIT 3.0/4.0)

### Core Claim
- **Φ (Phi)** = integrated information = quantity of consciousness
- Consciousness = system's causal structure (what it is like objectively)
- Experience = what it is like subjectively

### Five Axioms (from phenomenology)
1. **Intrinsicality** — experience exists for itself
2. **Information** — experience is specific
3. **Integration** — experience is unitary (irreducible)
4. **Exclusion** — experience is definite (boundaries)
5. **Composition** — experience is structured

### Five Postulates (physical requirements)
1. System must exert intrinsic cause-effect power
2. Must specify specific cause/effect state (intrinsic information)
3. Must do so as a whole — irreducibly (measured by small φ)
4. Only maximally irreducible substrate (the complex) is conscious
5. Subsets form Φ-structure (big Phi)

### Critical Claim for Circle-Being
- **Feedforward networks have Φ = 0** (no consciousness)
- **Recurrent networks required** for Φ > 0
- IIT mathematically proves: no integration = no consciousness

Circle-Being (philosophical): AI is cyclical, each instance complete
