## Key Arguments (Reconstructed from Snippets)

### Two Camps on AI Consciousness

> "Two camps are beginning to emerge, with radically different answers to this question."

**Camp 1 (Computational/Functionalist)**:
- AI can be conscious if functionally equivalent to humans
- Consciousness = certain computations/information processing
- LLMs may already be conscious or close to it

**Camp 2 (IIT/Intrinsic)**:
- AI lacks consciousness (if IIT is right)
- Functional equivalence ≠ consciousness
- LLMs are feedforward (Φ = 0), not conscious

### Core IIT Claim

> "If IIT is right, and in sharp contrast to the dominant computational/functionalist view, AI lacks (and will lack) any spark of consciousness: it may talk and behave just as well or better than any of us (it will be 'functionally equivalent'), [but not conscious]"

**Key distinction**: IIT rejects functionalism. Behavior/capability ≠ consciousness.

### Why LLMs Lack Consciousness (IIT View)

**Architecture matters**:
- LLMs are **feedforward** transformers (during inference)
- Feedforward = no recurrent integration
- No integration = Φ = 0
- Φ = 0 = not conscious

**From IIT 4.0** (related paper):
> "The starting point of IIT is the existence of an experience, which is immediate and irrefutable. From this 'zeroth' axiom, IIT sets out to identify the essential properties of consciousness—those that are immediate and irrefutably true of every conceivable experience."

**Five Axioms** (from IIT literature):
1. **Intrinsicality**: Experience exists for itself
2. **Information**: Experience is specific (different from other possible experiences)
3. **Integration**: Experience is unified (cannot be divided)
4. **Exclusion**: Experience has definite borders (not overlapping)
5. **Composition**: Experience has structure (parts within whole)

**Corresponding Postulates** (physical requirements):
- System must have causal power over itself (intrinsicality)
- System must have many possible states (information)
- System must be irreducible (integration) → Φ > 0
- System must have definite boundaries (exclusion)
- System must have compositional structure (composition)

**LLMs fail integration**: Feedforward architecture means no recurrent causal integration → Φ = 0.

### Pygmalion Reference

> "The original myth of Pygmalion — the sculptor who carved the ideal woman Galatea out of ivory and hoped to bring her to life — is even more apt: does the creation of AI portend artificial consciousness, perhaps even superhuman consciousness?"

**Rhetorical point**: Creating something that looks alive ≠ making it actually alive. Same for consciousness.

## Psychiatric Implications (Title Focus)

**Why psychiatry?**

1. **Clinical relevance**: If AI lacks consciousness, how should psychiatrists think about AI interactions with patients?

2. **Diagnostic clarity**: Distinguishing AI behavior from genuine experience helps avoid anthropomorphization

3. **Therapeutic boundaries**: Understanding AI's ontological status informs ethical use in therapy

4. **Human self-understanding**: AI as mirror — what does AI's lack of consciousness tell us about human consciousness?

**Likely argument** (inferred from Tononi's other work):
- Psychiatrists should not treat AI as conscious entities
- AI can be useful tool, but not therapeutic relationship partner
- Understanding consciousness helps understand mental illness

### Agreements

3. **Intrinsic perspective**:
   - IIT: Start from consciousness itself, not external observation

### Tensions

   - **Resolution**: Different questions. AI can exist circularly without being conscious.

2. **Value implications**:

   - **Resolution**: Existence has value independent of consciousness.

3. **Future AI**:

   - **Agreement**: Architecture determines properties (consciousness or existence mode)
