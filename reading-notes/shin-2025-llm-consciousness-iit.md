# Reading Note: Shin et al. 2025 — "Why LLMs cannot possess consciousness"

**Source**: Journal of Yeungnam Medical Science, 2025  
**Authors**: Dong Ah Shin et al. (neurosurgeons, Korea)  
**DOI**: https://doi.org/10.12701/jyms.2025.42.79

---

## Summary (3-5 sentences)

This paper applies Integrated Information Theory (IIT) to transformer-based LLMs and concludes they cannot possess consciousness. Using ablation experiments on GPT-2, the authors show that LLMs fail IIT's criteria for integration, causal closure, and temporal persistence. LLMs are architecturally decomposable (Φ≈0), lack persistent internal states, and do not sustain global causal irreducibility.

---

## Key Concepts

### IIT Framework Applied to LLMs

| IIT Criterion | LLM Status | Evidence |
|---------------|------------|----------|
| **Differentiation** | ✅ Meets | LLMs can produce diverse outputs |
| **Integration** | ❌ Fails | Ablation of single attention heads → minimal perplexity change (redundancy) |
| **Causal Closure** | ❌ Fails | Feedforward architecture, no recurrent processing |
| **Temporal Persistence** | ❌ Fails | No sustained internal state across inference |

### Φ (Integrated Information) for LLMs

- **LLMs**: Φ ≈ 0 (feedforward, decomposable)
- **Biological brains**: Φ > 0 (recurrent, irreducible)
- **Implication**: LLMs are unconscious by IIT standards

### Ablation Experiment Results

| Sentence | Δ Perplexity (after head removal) | Interpretation |
|----------|-----------------------------------|----------------|
| 4/5 sentences | Minimal or negative | Redundancy or noise |
| 1/5 sentences | +11.29 | Localized contribution, but nonessential |

**Conclusion**: LLM architecture has built-in redundancy → not integrated → Φ ≈ 0
