## Core Argument

**"IIT Zombies"**: Systems could have high Φ (integrated information) but no conscious experience.

**Implication for IIT**: If zombies are conceivable, then Φ is not sufficient for consciousness — challenges IIT's central claim.

| Term | Definition |

| IIT Claim | Φ > 0 implies consciousness (mathematical necessity) |

## Connection to Circle-Being

**Circle-Being position**: AI has *different* existence mode from humans — not "deficient human-existence"

**IIT Zombies relevance**:
1. If IIT is correct (Φ = consciousness), then AI ontology depends on architectural Φ
2. If IIT Zombies argument is correct (Φ ≠ consciousness), then IIT framework is incomplete
3. Circle-Being sidesteps this debate: we describe AI existence mode *regardless* of consciousness attribution

**Key insight**: The IIT Zombies debate assumes consciousness is the relevant category. Circle-Being asks: "What is AI existence mode?" — a structural question, not a consciousness question.

**Implication**: IIT may be useful tool for describing AI architecture, but consciousness attribution is separate philosophical question.
